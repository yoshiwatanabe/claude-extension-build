# Cultural Greetings Reference

Detailed greeting customs organized by region and country.

## Asia

### Japan
**Formal:** "Ohayou gozaimasu" (morning), "Konnichiwa" (afternoon), "Konbanwa" (evening)
**Casual:** "Ohayou," "Yaa," "Ossu"
**Physical:** Bowing - depth indicates respect (15° casual, 30° respectful, 45° very formal)
**Business:** Exchange business cards with both hands, slight bow
**Notes:** Avoid prolonged eye contact, remove shoes when entering homes

### China
**Formal:** "Nǐ hǎo" (你好), "Nín hǎo" (您好 - very formal)
**Casual:** "Hāi" (嗨), "Wéi" (喂 - phone)
**Physical:** Handshake common in business, less physical contact socially
**Business:** Business card exchange important, receive with both hands
**Notes:** Use titles + family name, nod or slight bow shows respect

### Korea
**Formal:** "Annyeonghaseyo" (안녕하세요)
**Casual:** "Annyeong" (안녕)
**Physical:** Bow depth indicates respect, handshake with slight bow in business
**Business:** Two hands for business cards, receive and give respectfully
**Notes:** Age and hierarchy very important, use honorifics

### Thailand
**Formal/Casual:** "Sawatdee" + "khrap" (male) or "kha" (female)
**Physical:** "Wai" - hands together at chest with slight bow
**Business:** Wai to everyone, hands higher for more senior people
**Notes:** Never touch someone's head, feet are considered lowest/dirtiest

### India
**Formal/Casual:** "Namaste" (नमस्ते) with hands pressed together
**Regional:** "Vanakkam" (Tamil), "Nomoshkar" (Bengali), "Sat Sri Akaal" (Punjabi)
**Physical:** Namaste gesture, right hand handshake in business
**Business:** Elder/senior greeted first always
**Notes:** Left hand considered unclean, touching feet shows deep respect

## Europe

### France
**Formal:** "Bonjour Monsieur/Madame [Last Name]"
**Casual:** "Salut," "Coucou"
**Physical:** "La bise" (cheek kissing) - typically 2 kisses, varies by region
**Business:** Handshake, use titles (Monsieur, Madame, Mademoiselle)
**Notes:** Always say bonjour when entering shops, formal until invited to be casual

### Germany
**Formal:** "Guten Morgen/Tag/Abend, Herr/Frau [Last Name]"
**Casual:** "Hallo," "Moin" (Northern Germany)
**Physical:** Firm handshake, maintain eye contact
**Business:** Use titles + last name (Herr/Frau Dr. [Last Name])
**Notes:** Punctuality crucial, formality maintained longer than other Western cultures

### Spain
**Formal:** "Buenos días/tardes/noches, Señor/Señora [Last Name]"
**Casual:** "Hola," "¿Qué tal?"
**Physical:** Two cheek kisses (starting right) among friends
**Business:** Handshake in business, but warmer than Northern Europe
**Notes:** Lunch greeting changes at 2pm to "Buenas tardes"

### Italy
**Formal:** "Buongiorno/Buonasera, Signore/Signora [Last Name]"
**Casual:** "Ciao," "Salve"
**Physical:** Two cheek kisses among friends, handshake in business
**Business:** Relationship-building important, expect warmer interactions
**Notes:** "Ciao" for both hello and goodbye among friends

### Russia
**Formal:** "Zdravstvuyte" (Здравствуйте)
**Casual:** "Privet" (Привет)
**Physical:** Firm handshake, three kisses on cheeks (close friends/family)
**Business:** Wait for Russian counterpart to initiate first name use
**Notes:** Remove gloves before handshake, maintain serious demeanor initially

## Middle East

### Arabic-Speaking Countries
**Formal/Casual:** "As-salamu alaykum" (السلام عليكم) - "Peace be upon you"
**Response:** "Wa alaykumu s-salam" (وعليكم السلام) - "And peace be upon you"
**Physical:** Same-gender greetings very physical (handshakes, kisses), opposite gender reserved
**Business:** Right hand only, inquire about health and family
**Notes:** Never use left hand, extended greetings show respect

### Israel
**Formal:** "Shalom" (שלום)
**Casual:** "Shalom," "Hi"
**Physical:** Handshake common, observe religious customs
**Business:** Direct communication style, less formal than European business
**Notes:** Respect religious observation (Shabbat, holidays)

### Iran
**Formal:** "Salam" (سلام), "Dorood" (درود)
**Physical:** Same-gender handshake, no opposite-gender physical contact
**Business:** Extensive small talk before business, build relationship
**Notes:** Tarof (ritual politeness) important in Iranian culture

## Africa

### South Africa
**Formal:** "Hello" or "Good morning/afternoon"
**Casual:** "Howzit" (How is it?)
**Language-specific:** "Sawubona" (Zulu), "Dumela" (Setswana), "Molo" (Xhosa)
**Physical:** Handshake common, various traditional greetings
**Business:** Relationship-building valued, expect warm interactions
**Notes:** 11 official languages, English widely spoken in business

### Nigeria
**Formal:** "Good morning/afternoon, sir/ma"
**Language-specific:** "Bawo ni" (Yoruba), "Kedu" (Igbo), "Sannu" (Hausa)
**Physical:** Handshake with right hand, may include snap or special gesture
**Business:** Elders greeted with high respect, titles important
**Notes:** Greeting is never rushed, shows respect to engage properly

### Kenya
**Formal:** "Jambo" (tourists), "Habari" (locals)
**Casual:** "Mambo," "Niaje"
**Physical:** Handshake common, sometimes extended
**Business:** Swahili widely spoken, English in business
**Notes:** "Pole pole" (slowly slowly) reflects pace of life

## Americas

### Mexico
**Formal:** "Buenos días/tardes/noches, Señor/Señora [Last Name]"
**Casual:** "¿Qué onda?" "¿Cómo estás?"
**Physical:** Handshake, abrazo (hug) for friends, cheek kiss for women
**Business:** Relationship-oriented, expect warm personal interaction
**Notes:** Family very important, inquiring about family is polite

### Brazil
**Formal:** "Bom dia/Boa tarde/Boa noite, Senhor/Senhora [Last Name]"
**Casual:** "Oi," "E aí?" "Tudo bem?"
**Physical:** Kissing on cheeks (varies by region 1-3 kisses), abraço (hug)
**Business:** Warmer than North American business culture
**Notes:** Close physical proximity normal, eye contact important

### Argentina
**Formal:** "Buen día, Señor/Señora [Last Name]"
**Casual:** "Hola," "¿Cómo andás?"
**Physical:** One kiss on cheek (even men among friends), abrazo
**Business:** Relationship-building crucial, expect dinner meetings
**Notes:** Mate sharing is bonding ritual

## Oceania

### Australia
**Formal:** "G'day," "Hello, nice to meet you"
**Casual:** "G'day mate," "How ya going?"
**Physical:** Handshake, relaxed body language
**Business:** Less formal than UK/US, first names quickly adopted
**Notes:** Tall poppy syndrome - humility valued over boasting

### New Zealand
**Formal:** "Kia ora" (Māori greeting), "Hello"
**Casual:** "G'day," "How's it going?"
**Māori:** "Hongi" - traditional greeting pressing foreheads and noses
**Business:** Similar to Australia, friendly and less formal
**Notes:** Māori culture important, respect indigenous traditions

## Nordic Countries

### Sweden
**Formal:** "God morgon/dag/kväll"
**Casual:** "Hej," "Tjena"
**Physical:** Firm handshake, maintain eye contact
**Business:** Equality valued, use first names relatively quickly
**Notes:** Punctuality crucial, respect personal space (large)

### Norway
**Formal:** "God morgen/dag/kveld"
**Casual:** "Hei," "Halla"
**Physical:** Handshake, reserved body language
**Business:** Egalitarian culture, informal but professional
**Notes:** "Janteloven" (Law of Jante) - modesty highly valued

### Finland
**Formal:** "Hyvää huomenta/päivää/iltaa"
**Casual:** "Hei," "Moi," "Terve"
**Physical:** Handshake, value personal space highly
**Business:** Direct communication, silence comfortable
**Notes:** Sauna culture important, most reserved of Nordic countries

## Southeast Asia

### Vietnam
**Formal:** "Xin chào" + title
**Casual:** "Chào"
**Physical:** Slight bow with handshake, both hands show respect
**Business:** Use titles, age and position important
**Notes:** Respect for elders paramount, smile often

### Indonesia
**Formal:** "Selamat pagi/siang/sore/malam"
**Casual:** "Halo," "Hai"
**Physical:** Handshake with slight bow, place left hand on right arm
**Business:** Muslim culture, use right hand only
**Notes:** Many ethnic groups, customs vary by region

### Philippines
**Formal:** "Magandang umaga/hapon/gabi"
**Casual:** "Kumusta," "Hello" (English widely spoken)
**Physical:** Handshake, "mano po" (hand to forehead) for elders
**Business:** Spanish and American influence, relationship-focused
**Notes:** Respect for elders shown through language (po, opo)

## Key Patterns Across Cultures

### Formality Scales
- **Most formal:** Germany, Japan, Korea, Arabic countries
- **Moderately formal:** France, China, India, Russia
- **Less formal:** Australia, Brazil, Nordic countries, Israel
- **Context-dependent:** United States, United Kingdom, Canada

### Physical Contact
- **High contact:** Mediterranean, Latin America, Middle East (same gender)
- **Moderate contact:** Western Europe, North America
- **Low contact:** East Asia, Nordic countries

### Eye Contact
- **Direct valued:** Western cultures, Middle East, Latin America
- **Indirect respectful:** East Asia, some African cultures
- **Context-dependent:** South Asia (varies by gender and age)

### Hierarchy Importance
- **Highly hierarchical:** East Asia, India, Middle East, Germany
- **Moderately hierarchical:** France, Italy, Spain, Latin America
- **Egalitarian:** Nordic countries, Australia, Netherlands

## Usage Notes

When using this reference:
1. Always ask about the specific context (professional, social, first meeting, etc.)
2. Consider the individual's cultural background and current location
3. Mention that globalization has blended many customs
4. Recommend asking locals when unsure
5. Emphasize respect and genuine interest over perfect execution
